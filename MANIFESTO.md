# The Foundry Manifesto

We're not building a dashboard.

We're building a workshop for people who forge their own teams.

---

## We refuse

**The seat-license enterprise dashboard.** $500 a month for the privilege of
watching numbers change. Every feature buried three menus deep. Every setting
demanding a call with a Customer Success Manager. That's not a tool — that's a
tax.

**AI slop that treats you like a browser tab.** One giant text box. Type into
the void. Hope. No accountability, no team, no memory of what you told it
yesterday, no way to run five things in parallel. A chat window is not an
operating system.

**The generic AI SaaS aesthetic.** Purple gradients. Inter body font. Rounded
white cards. "AI-powered." "Reimagining." "The future of work." Every one of
them looks like every other one. If your product looks like the template,
you *are* the template.

**Employee-replacement marketing.** We don't sell "fire your marketing team."
We sell a warm workshop for the founder who never had one to begin with.
Different tribe. Better tribe.

---

## We stand for

**One screen. Your whole team.** Twelve workers on the Floor. Each with their
own memory, their own persona, their own model. You know their names, you
type shortcuts you learned by osmosis, and you can see what every one of them
is doing without switching a tab.

**Warm tools for real work.** A Michelin menu price for the money moment. Serif
where it counts. Amber-on-dark instead of another cold blue. Two o'clock in
the morning, the fire in the Foundry still lit.

**Operator-owned, not vendor-controlled.** Your data lives in one JSON file on
your disk. Your Claude account, your keys, your machine, your rules. If we
disappear tomorrow, your workshop still runs.

**Zero build step.** Two files: `server.js` and `public/`. Read the source in
one sitting. Fork it, break it, rewrite it. No node_modules black box.

**Restraint over rainbow.** One accent color used sparingly. One serif for
display. One sans for body. One mono for numbers. Four levels of surface,
zero drop shadows. If you can't scan the interface in three seconds, we
messed up.

**The Ledger tells the truth.** Every token. Every dollar. Every turn. Plan
vs. API separated so you know exactly what your Claude subscription covered
and what came out of pocket.

---

## Who this is for

You already have too much to do. You're one person, maybe with a contractor.
You've tried the enterprise suite, the "AI assistant," the fifteen-tab
Chrome-window productivity system, and none of them let you actually ship.

You want a room where you can walk in at 6am with a rough brief and walk out
at noon with copy shipped, ads live, and a landing page in review. You'd
rather forge your own team than pay $500/user/month for the privilege of
someone else's opinion of a workflow.

You're the operator. You're the founder. You're the agency of one.

If that sounds like you, welcome to the Foundry.

---

## What we mean when we say it

- **The Foundry** — the app. Your workshop.
- **The Floor** — where your workers stand.
- **The Grid** — every worker, side by side, one glance.
- **The Focus** — one worker fullscreen, deep work.
- **The Wire** — live activity, always on.
- **The Ledger** — the full history, every dollar, every token.
- **The Bell (⌘K)** — one shortcut, every action.
- **The Anvil** — where the Director's assignments get hammered into
  dispatches.
- **Muster** — call every worker at once.
- **Blueprints** — reusable teams. Marketing, e-commerce, SaaS, coaching,
  real estate. Save your own.
- **Forge a mission** — dispatch a task.
- **Hire a worker** — add an agent.
- **Founder** — you. Also (in a later version) the paid tier.

These are ours now. They mean something specific inside this workshop, and
you're an insider the moment you use them out loud with another operator who
also runs a Foundry.
